I’m a Technical Program Manager focused on systems, data, and globalization.

Most of my work sits in the gaps between teams: where product decisions meet real-world constraints, where data models break down in practice, and where global requirements surface issues that don’t show up in a single market. I spend a lot of time translating between perspectives—engineering, product, and customer-facing teams—so that decisions hold up under real usage, not just in design.

My background is in localization and international product development, including working directly with Japanese stakeholders and customers. That experience shaped how I approach program management: I assume misalignment exists until it’s proven otherwise, and I try to surface it early, when it’s still fixable.

The pieces in this portfolio reflect how I work through problems:
- breaking down complex systems so non-specialists can use them
- identifying failure points that metrics alone don’t reveal
- designing processes that scale across teams and regions
- making tradeoffs explicit so they can be managed, not discovered late

I’m particularly interested in roles that involve:
- distributed systems and data workflows  
- global product development  
- cross-functional programs with unclear ownership  
- situations where the “right” solution isn’t obvious at the start  

If that overlaps with what you’re working on, I’d be glad to talk.
